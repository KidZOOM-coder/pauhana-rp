RULES

PAU HANA RP ‚Äî Server Guidelines

These rules exist to keep roleplay immersive, fair, and enjoyable for everyone.

Notice Center

Stay ahead of the rulebook. Your knowledge and professionalism directly affect outcomes in tickets and penalties.
  • Accountability matters. Actions taken both IC and OOC are considered when STAFF review situations and make decisions.
  • Rule awareness is expected. Players are responsible for knowing the RULES ‚Äî not being aware of one does NOT EXCUSE A VIOLATION.
  • Communication comes first. STAFF will always aim to explain and clarify, but enforcement will be applied when needed to maintain roleplay quality.
  • No outside promotion. Advertising or directing players to other servers or communities is NOT ALLOWED.
  • Reports must be timely. Incidents should be reported within 24 hours to be considered.
  • Zero-tolerance content. Content involving explicit material, self-harm, or extremist themes is STRICTLY PROHIBITED and may result in immediate action.

Global Policies - Server Rules

Use of Clips & Footage

We do not allow players to submit footage that is not their own unless permission is granted by the original creator. This includes livestream clips, recordings, or third-party platforms. This policy prevents harassment and meta-gaming.

Staff Authority & Burden of Proof

Staff reserve the right to punish rule violations. It is your responsibility to provide evidence when making claims. Saying ‚Äújust check the logs‚Äù is not valid proof.

Handling RP Rule Breaks

If a rule is broken in RP, staff appreciate when players handle the situation discreetly. This helps preserve immersion and prevents unnecessary escalation.

Files, Purchases & Ownership

Any file or purchase provided to the city is permanently released. Purchases remain with the buyer unless they are key-master files. Disbanded organizations forfeit ownership.

1-of-1 Items & Inactivity

Any 1-of-1 item is forfeited if the owner is banned or inactive for 120+ days without an approved LOA. Items may be released back into the community.

Meaningful RP Before Lethal Force

Before using lethal force, at least one full minute of meaningful roleplay must occur. Clear escalation and justification are required. Random violence is considered Fail RP.

Toxicity & Respect

Pau Hana RP is an inclusive community built on respect, fairness, and mature roleplay. These expectations exist to ensure a safe, fair, and immersive environment for everyone.

Respect Toward Others

You must remain respectful toward all players and administrators at all times ‚Äî both in the city and in Discord. Pau Hana RP includes people from many cultures, beliefs, and backgrounds. Respect is required in every interaction, with no exceptions.

Prohibited Slurs & Terms

Pau Hana RP does NOT allow the use of sexist, racist, homophobic, or transphobic language. Additionally, no terms targeting individuals with disabilities or special needs are permitted. Violations will result in disciplinary action.

Sexually Derogatory Language

There is a zero-tolerance policy for sexually demeaning or derogatory language aimed at any sex. This includes words such as ‚Äúwhore‚Äù, ‚Äúslut‚Äù, or similar phrases ‚Äî whether used jokingly, casually, or in character.

Violations result in a minimum of 250 Community Service. Know your audience.

Harassment in Roleplay

Harassment includes any behavior that makes another participant feel uncomfortable, threatened, intimidated, or disrespected. This applies to both verbal interactions and in-character actions.

Rape RP Is Strictly Forbidden
Rape RP is never allowed ‚Äî including jokes, threats, implications, or scenes involving sexual violence.
Suicide RP Restrictions
Suicide RP is not allowed unless it is part of a voluntary, final permanent kill of your own character. No other forms are permitted.
Pregnancy RP Not Allowed
Pregnancy RP is strictly prohibited in all forms. This includes attempts to imply, portray, or reference pregnancy in RP scenes.

OOC & IC INTERACTIONS

PAU HANA RP requires players to remain in character at all times unless directly instructed by staff. Your immersion ‚Äî and the immersion of others ‚Äî depends on maintaining the line between IC and OOC.

Going OOC (Out of Character) is prohibited.
This includes comments about rules, tickets, meta topics, evidence, or anything that breaks immersion.

DO NOT GO OOC

  • Do not break character in any situation.
  • Do not reveal personal or private information.
  • Only interact OOC when staff explicitly instruct you.

WHAT COUNTS AS GOING OOC

  • Discussing rules or mechanics in RP.
  • Talking about tickets, reports, or clips.
  • Implying staff actions or enforcement IC.

CHARACTER BOUNDARIES & RP LIMITATIONS

  • You may not perma-kill another player‚Äôs character.
  • Characters cannot share knowledge or assets without approval.
  • On your side, you may speak but must act injured and stay local.
  • Flatbacked players may not speak or use radio.
  • Do not reference tickets or staff unless staff are present IC.

New Life Rule (NLR)

The New Life Rule controls what your character remembers and how you behave after being downed or ‚Äúgoing to the light.‚Äù These rules apply to everyone, including PD and EMS.

1
If you go to the light, NLR is in effect. You are considered dead for that situation and must forget everything leading up to your death.
2
If you are flatback, you are considered dead for that scene and cannot talk at all. Body cam footage cannot be used to remember events. PD may use footage for investigative purposes only.
3
You cannot go to the light while being transported to the hospital if you are in EMS or PD custody.
4
If EMS is unavailable and PD is on scene, PD must attempt life-saving measures. You cannot go to the light while CPR is being performed. If CPR has stopped for 30 seconds or more, you may choose to go to the light.
5
If revived, you are considered out of the current scene. You must leave with your hands up and may not rejoin until the scene fully clears.
6
The following actions are considered rejoining / re-injecting:
  • Leaving a scene and returning to participate again.
  • Disconnecting and returning on a different character.
  • Driving back and forth after abandoning an RP scene.
  • Transporting bodies and returning to involve yourself.

Meta Gaming

Meta gaming is using information gained outside of roleplay to influence in-character decisions. This breaks immersion and is strictly prohibited.

1
Using information from streams, Discord, chats, or out-of-game sources to benefit your character is meta gaming.
2
You may not reference server mechanics, scripts, bugs, or systems in roleplay unless explicitly allowed by staff.
3
Knowledge gained from previous characters does not carry over to new characters.
4
Watching another player‚Äôs stream and acting on information seen there ‚Äî even indirectly ‚Äî is considered hard meta gaming.
5
You may not use death screens, kill feeds, map markers, or UI elements as in-character information.
6
If you accidentally receive out-of-character information, you are expected to ignore it completely and continue roleplay naturally.

Power Gaming

Power gaming is forcing outcomes, ignoring roleplay limits, or using unrealistic actions to gain an advantage. This behavior breaks immersion and fairness.

1
Forcing actions onto other players without giving them a chance to respond is considered power gaming.
2
Ignoring injuries, restraints, fear, or physical limitations to continue fighting or escaping is not allowed.
3
Unrealistic combat actions such as instantly breaking free, tanking damage, or overpowering multiple players without RP justification is prohibited.
4
You may not use game mechanics or animations to bypass roleplay outcomes or avoid consequences.
5
Escaping custody, restraints, or scenes without valid roleplay escalation is considered power gaming.
6
Power gaming violations may result in warnings, scene reversals, or escalated punishment depending on severity.

Combat Logging

Combat logging is leaving the server or disconnecting to avoid roleplay, consequences, or active situations. This is taken seriously and strictly enforced.

1
Disconnecting during or immediately after a hostile situation, chase, arrest, or confrontation is considered combat logging.
2
Logging out while restrained, injured, being pursued, or actively involved in an RP scene is not allowed.
3
If you crash or disconnect unintentionally, you are required to rejoin immediately and continue the situation.
4
Failure to return after a disconnect must be reported to staff as soon as possible with valid reasoning.
5
Repeated or intentional combat logging may result in escalated punishment, including temporary or permanent removal.

Gun Fear

Gun fear represents realistic reactions to lethal threats. When a firearm is present, characters must respond with appropriate caution and survival instinct.

1
When a firearm is drawn and aimed at you, your character must show fear and compliance appropriate to the situation.
2
Ignoring a lethal threat, taunting, or acting unrealistically calm while under gunpoint is not allowed.
3
Attempting to fight, flee, or disarm someone with a firearm must be clearly justified through roleplay and circumstance.
4
Drawing a weapon against an armed opponent without RP escalation may be considered power gaming.
5
Repeated failure to show gun fear can result in staff action or escalation in punishment.

Criminal / Cop Interactions

Criminals and law enforcement must interact in a way that is realistic, fair, and enjoyable for everyone involved. These rules define expectations during traffic stops, chases, robberies, and active scenes.

  • 1
    When pulled over, you must roleplay the traffic stop for at least 1 full minute. PD must also create a realistic traffic stop scenario in return.
  • 2
    If you were not part of the original heist, robbery, or traffic stop, and no outside help was demanded, you are NOT ALLOWED to interfere.

    If outside help is requested:
    • They count toward the total criminal count.
    • PD must be informed that outside help is involved.
  • 3
    You may not ambush PD unless it occurs within your gang‚Äôs compound or controlled turf. Officers must be warned before entering controlled turf.

    Only your own gang may assist ‚Äî no random contracts.
  • 4
    Dirty cops are NOT allowed. Selling, giving, or abusing PD-issued equipment is a bannable offense.

    This includes:
    • Removing items from evidence to give away
    • Deleting MDT entries to cover crimes
    Mistakes or policy violations may still be handled through PD‚Äôs internal RP systems and admin review.
  • 5
    You may take a cop hostage only when there are 6 or more officers on duty. This must involve proper escalation ‚Äî not rushing with guns.

    Once taken hostage, the officer must go off duty for the duration. PD must fear for hostages‚Äô lives, including EMS hostages.
  • 6
    If there are fewer than 4 cops on duty, criminals must warn officers at least 3 separate times to leave the scene before any loss of life for PD may occur.
  • 7
    If criminals take a body cam or dash cam from PD, that officer loses all information from the incident for future investigations or court cases.
  • 8
    Cops and cop vehicles may be looted ‚Äî but NOT for weapons or any PD-only equipment (this includes MedKits).

    Examples of allowed loot:
    • Food
    • Radios
    • Phones
    • Ammo
    • Cleaning kits
    • Tasers
    • Bandages
    • Weapon attachments
    • Evidence left on the officer
  • 9
    If PD enters an active shootout, they may be shot without additional instigation ‚Äî the scene is already active.

    However, criminals cannot shoot officers who are not driving into, approaching, or actively attempting to engage in that scene.

EMS Roleplay (EMS RP)

EMS roleplay is required to be realistic, respectful, and immersive. Medical roleplay impacts life-or-death situations and must be taken seriously at all times by both EMS and civilians.

  • 1
    EMS must provide realistic and professional medical roleplay when responding to scenes. Rushing treatment or ignoring injuries is not allowed.
  • 2
    Civilians must comply with EMS instructions while receiving treatment. Ignoring medical direction without RP justification is considered Fail RP.
  • 3
    If you are downed or injured, you must roleplay your injuries appropriately. You may not sprint, fight, or act uninjured unless cleared by EMS.
  • 4
    EMS are considered neutral parties. Targeting, kidnapping, or killing EMS without strong roleplay justification is strictly prohibited.
  • 5
    EMS vehicles, equipment, and personnel may NOT be looted under any circumstances unless explicitly approved by staff for a storyline.
  • 6
    EMS may refuse transport or treatment if a patient is actively hostile or the scene is unsafe. Scene safety must be established before care begins.
  • 7
    If a patient is transported to the hospital, they are considered in medical custody and may not leave until cleared by EMS or transferred to PD custody.
  • 8
    EMS must follow New Life Rule (NLR) guidelines and may not use medical knowledge to restore memory or reinsert a player into an active scene.
  • 9
    Abuse, harassment, or trolling of EMS staff ‚Äî including refusing RP or wasting resources ‚Äî may result in administrative action.

Helicopters & Vehicles

Vehicle usage plays a major role in pursuits, escapes, and scene control. These rules exist to maintain realism, balance, and fair roleplay for all parties.

  • 1
    Vehicles must be operated realistically and responsibly. Unrealistic driving, ignoring vehicle damage, or treating vehicles as disposable is considered Fail RP.
  • 2
    Using vehicles to ram, pin, or intentionally crush players without proper escalation or justification is not allowed. Vehicles are not weapons unless the situation clearly escalates to that level.
  • 3
    Jumping vehicles off extreme heights, cliffs, or obstacles without consequence or injury roleplay is prohibited. You must account for vehicle condition and character injury.
  • 4
    Helicopters may only be used when properly staffed and roleplayed. Solo or reckless helicopter use that disrupts RP flow may result in staff action.
  • 5
    Using helicopters to hover directly over scenes, spam spotlighting, or force unrealistic outcomes is not allowed. Air units must maintain safe distance and believable flight behavior.
  • 6
    Landing helicopters in unsafe or unrealistic locations (crowded streets, rooftops without clearance, active scenes) without proper setup and escalation is prohibited.
  • 7
    Vehicles may not be used to bypass roleplay outcomes, avoid consequences, or escape situations without valid escalation and opportunity for counterplay.
  • 8
    Vehicle and aircraft abuse ‚Äî including repeated reckless use, intentional crashing, or exploiting mechanics ‚Äî may result in vehicle restrictions or removal.

Tsunami ‚Äî Server Restart Protocol

Tsunamis represent server restarts and maintenance cycles. These rules exist to prevent exploitation, rushed scenarios, and immersion-breaking behavior.

  • 1
    A tsunami is announced in advance through in-game notifications or official communication. Players are expected to prepare to safely conclude ongoing roleplay.
  • 2
    During the final minutes before a tsunami, players may not initiate new roleplay scenarios, crimes, pursuits, or confrontations.
  • 3
    Ongoing scenes must be paused or wrapped up naturally. Forced conclusions, rushed actions, or ‚Äúlast-minute plays‚Äù are not permitted.
  • 4
    Players may not use the tsunami to escape arrest, death, injury, or consequences. Any scenario in progress must be continued after restart if applicable.
  • 5
    Logging out early to avoid RP, hide assets, or reposition characters before a tsunami is considered combat logging or fail RP.
  • 6
    After a tsunami, players should resume roleplay logically and respect injuries, locations, and outcomes from prior scenes unless staff directs otherwise.
  • 7
    Staff may intervene, pause, or nullify scenarios affected by a tsunami if fairness or stability is compromised. Staff decisions regarding tsunami-related incidents are final.

Killing & Robbing

Killing and robbing are high-impact actions that must be driven by meaningful roleplay, proper escalation, and clear intent. Random violence or robbery without justification is strictly prohibited.

  • 1
    Killing another player requires clear roleplay escalation and a valid in-character reason. Random or unprovoked killing is considered RDM (Random Deathmatch).
  • 2
    Robbing another player must involve meaningful interaction and communication. Immediately shooting or incapacitating a player solely to loot them without roleplay is not allowed.
  • 3
    You may not rob players in Green Zones or during protected roleplay scenarios (medical treatment, active EMS care, or staff-supervised scenes).
  • 4
    Killing to prevent reporting, identification, or retaliation must be clearly justified through roleplay and circumstance. Executions without escalation are not permitted.
  • 5
    Downed or incapacitated players may not be executed or ‚Äúfinished off‚Äù unless the situation clearly escalates to that level and falls within active Red Zone conditions.
  • 6
    You may rob items that are visibly carried or reasonably accessible during the scene. Robbing excessive or unrelated items without roleplay justification may be considered abuse.
  • 7
    Robbing the same player repeatedly within a short timeframe without new escalation or storyline development is considered harassment or powergaming.
  • 8
    Killing or robbing solely to gain money, loot, or convenience without story-driven motivation is not valid roleplay.
  • 9
    Staff reserve the right to review intent, escalation, and context when determining rule compliance. Punishments may scale based on severity and repetition.

Whitelisted Jobs

Whitelisted jobs represent trusted roles that directly affect city balance, progression, and immersion. Players in these roles are held to a higher standard of conduct and roleplay quality.

  • 1
    Players in whitelisted roles must maintain realistic, professional roleplay at all times while on duty. Abuse of authority or power is not tolerated.
  • 2
    Whitelisted jobs may not be used to benefit personal characters, friends, or organizations unfairly. Any form of favoritism or corruption without approved storyline is prohibited.
  • 3
    You may not perform off-duty actions that conflict with your whitelisted role unless explicitly approved by staff. This includes criminal activity that undermines your position.
  • 4
    Whitelisted roles must follow department policies, SOPs, and command structure where applicable. Internal discipline should be handled through roleplay before staff escalation.
  • 5
    Using whitelisted tools, permissions, or systems to bypass mechanics, avoid consequences, or gain unfair advantage is considered powergaming.
  • 6
    Inactivity, repeated low-effort roleplay, or failure to meet expectations may result in whitelist removal with or without prior warning.
  • 7
    Players removed from a whitelisted job may be required to wait a cooldown period before reapplying. Reinstatement is at staff discretion.
  • 8
    Staff reserve the right to review conduct, performance, and intent when determining eligibility to retain or hold a whitelisted role. Staff decisions regarding whitelisted jobs are final.

Banks & Robberies

Robberies are high-stakes, coordinated roleplay scenarios. These rules exist to ensure fair escalation, clear communication, and enjoyable outcomes for both criminals and law enforcement.

  • 1
    All robberies must involve clear roleplay intent and planning. Rushing objectives, speed-running mechanics, or bypassing interaction is considered Fail RP.
  • 2
    Hostages must be obtained and roleplayed properly. Fake hostages, NPC-only hostages (when players are available), or rushed hostage grabs without RP are not permitted.
  • 3
    Robberies may not be initiated in Green Zones or during protected scenarios unless explicitly allowed by staff for a storyline or event.
  • 4
    Criminals must allow reasonable negotiation time with PD. Immediately opening fire or forcing outcomes without escalation is not allowed.
  • 5
    PD response and tactics should scale appropriately to the threat level. Overwhelming force without escalation may be reviewed by staff.
  • 6
    Using robberies to bait or farm PD kills, create repetitive shootouts, or force constant engagements is prohibited.
  • 7
    Escape plans must be realistic and roleplayed. Instant vehicle swaps, teleporting, or exploiting mechanics to evade consequences is not allowed.
  • 8
    If a robbery fails, criminals must accept the outcome. Disconnecting, stalling, or forcing resets to avoid arrest or loss is considered combat logging.
  • 9
    Robbery frequency, group size, and escalation limits may be adjusted by staff to maintain city balance and server health.

PC Checks

PC Checks are conducted to ensure fair play, rule compliance, and the integrity of roleplay. These checks are not punitive by default and are used when staff require clarification during investigations.

  • 1
    Players may be asked to participate in a PC Check during or after an investigation. Cooperation is required unless otherwise stated by staff.
  • 2
    Refusing, delaying, or intentionally interfering with a PC Check without valid reasoning may result in disciplinary action.
  • 3
    PC Checks are limited in scope. Staff will only request access to relevant files, settings, or information necessary for the investigation.
  • 4
    Players are not required to provide personal files unrelated to FiveM, GTA, or the incident being reviewed. Staff will not access private or sensitive personal data.
  • 5
    Tampering with files, deleting data, or altering settings before or during a PC Check is prohibited and may result in escalated punishment.
  • 6
    PC Checks may include reviewing FiveM settings, overlays, configs, keybinds, macros, or third-party software relevant to the investigation.
  • 7
    If a violation is found, staff will explain the findings and outcome. Punishment will be based on severity, intent, and history.
  • 8
    Abuse of the PC Check process ‚Äî by either players or staff ‚Äî is not tolerated. Any concerns should be addressed through proper staff channels.

Use Tickets

Tickets should be used for minor or clear-cut rule violations that do not require extended investigation.

Use Reports

Reports should be submitted for serious, repeated, or complex incidents that require evidence review or staff discussion.

SOPs ‚Äî Standard Operating Procedures

Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) define roleplay expectations for all players. SOPs exist to maintain consistency, realism, and fair staff enforcement across the city.

  • Civilians must value their life at all times.
  • Random violence, vigilantism, or escalation without RP buildup is prohibited.
  • Fear RP is required when faced with weapons or overwhelming force.
  • Civilians may not interfere with active police scenes unless roleplay permits.
  • Unrealistic behavior (suicide rushing, powergaming, NVL) is not allowed.
  • DISCLAIMER: If you are an approved gang you must involve yourself in criminal activities including kidnappings, robberies, shootings etc. If you do not involve yourself in any gang activities or interact with other gangs your gang can risk disbandment. If you don't want to do gang activities and be in shootings, live the civilian life. If you‚Äôre not about that life this is not the SOP for you.
  • Criminal actions must have realistic planning and RP justification.
  • Gang activity must follow established turf and escalation rules.
  • No excessive violence without reason or continuation RP.
  • Robberies, kidnappings, and ambushes must allow counter-play.
  • Combat logging or avoiding consequences is strictly prohibited.
  • Whitelisted roles must prioritize roleplay over winning.
  • Abuse of authority or equipment will result in escalation.
  • EMS must provide medical RP before revives when possible.
  • Police must follow escalation, force, and engagement SOPs.
  • Corruption RP requires staff approval.
  • Staff decisions are final during active situations.
  • Rule disputes must be handled through reports, not in RP.
  • Staff discretion applies in situations not explicitly covered.
  • Abuse of loopholes will be treated as rule violations.
  • Repeated SOP violations escalate punishment severity.
SOP violations may result in warnings, community service, suspensions, or permanent removal depending on severity.
